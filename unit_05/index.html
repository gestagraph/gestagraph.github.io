Examples

  • Sherrie has a collection of her mother and grandmother's handwritten recipies. Her children and grandchildren are low-key fighting over who will take over these precious family heirlooms. Sherrie wants a way to share them, paired with photos and the family history. She decidess she wants to share them electronically by creating a password-protected blog that only the family can access, but she doesn't know how. GestaGraph helps Sherrie develop a plan, and decide on a platform; provides technical support as Sherrie becomes self-sufficient with her blog; and when Sherrie decides she also wants to turn this Project into a hard-copy book, GestaGraph coaches Sherrie through the process of selecting a print-on-demand provider and preparing project for printing.
  • As a young man, Daniel joined the French Resistence to the Nazi invasion. Daniel has stories he's never been able to fully tell. Daniel wants his personal role in history written down, just to know that his view on truth of that time won't be lost to history. Daniel isn't up to doing the typing himself: he wants GestaGraph to sit with him, ask questions, and record the answers.
